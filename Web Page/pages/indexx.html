Welcome to Benguet Province!


The Philippines is not just famous for its islands and beaches. The country also boasts of natural and scenic landscapes with the province of Benguet housing a number of them. Found at the southernmost part of the Cordillera Administrative Region in North of Luzon, Benguet is nicknamed the Salad Bowl of the Philippines, since it's one of the main source of fresh vegetables in the country. With picturesque mountain views, tranquil atmosphere, and cool breeze, Benguet provides a refreshing escape from the busy city life.




See and Do
Benguet is most known for being the home of Baguio City, a top tourist destination that is also called the Summer Capital of the Philippines. You can hang out in Baguio tourist spots like Camp John Hay, Burnham Park, and The Mansion among many others. Benguet's capital is La Trinidad from where the production of vegetables and strawberries come from. Visitors may take the leisure of picking their own bunch of the freshest strawberries and vegetables at the farm for a fair price. Since La Trinidad is abundant in strawberries, they have treates in strawberry-flavor and are a must-try like strawberry taho (hot silken tofu with sweet syrup and sago perls), strawberry ice cream, and strawberry jam. Check out the hillside community of Stonehill, Botiwtiw and Sadjap (STOBOSA) in La Trinidad is composed of colorful houses, reminiscent of the favela murals of Brazil's Rio de Janeiro. For nature-lovers, take a hike at Mount Pulag to see the fascinating sea of clouds. Every step you take to reach the top will surely be worth it as soon as you see the awe-inspiring view.

Where to Stay
From budget-friendly to luxurious, hotels and inns are mostly centralized in Baguio since the city is a popular tourist destination in Benguet.
